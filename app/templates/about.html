Project Description

"Flowering Phenology" is the biological timing of the reproductive process of angiosperms. Changes in flowering time can mean changes in species fitness, community distribution, and can even affect species beyond plants, such as the pollinators which depend on these flowers.

With this project, we hoped to investigate how the changing California climate may affect the flowering phenology of native and non-native species, and how that may affect the distribution and community makeup of these species in differing divisions of California.

While we did not get to comparing California Climate as of yet (03/18/22), we have so far created an aggregated database that you may query data a look at through plotly's attractive visualizations.

Data

For the flowering observation data, we downloaded species data from iNaturalist and catergoried them as native or non-native according to Cal flora. One assumption we made is that the date of every observation off of its flowering date.
You can download the reference list here.

As for the climate data, it was sourced from the SC ACIS sub-team of NOAA. It is an aggregated climate database of the US.
